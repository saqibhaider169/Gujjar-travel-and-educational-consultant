Why Study In New Zealand?

New Zealand ranks third globally in education, offering world-class credentials, abundant research opportunities, and an exceptional quality of life. Although, studying here means enjoying stunning beaches, diverse landscapes, and a vibrant outdoor lifestyle. Furthermore, embrace a unique, multicultural society that welcomes all talents, backgrounds, and beliefs.

Moreover, students in New Zealand are encouraged to think critically, collaborate, and innovate. With top-notch education, state-of-the-art labs, and highly qualified instructors, you have everything you need to achieve your goals. New Zealand is an exciting place to learn and grow, blending academic excellence with breathtaking natural beauty.

Admission Requirements In New Zealand

  • A valid passport
  • Personal Interview
  • Updated Resume or CV
  • New Zealand Student Visa
  • Proof of English Proficiency (IELTS/TOEFL)
  • 500-word Statement of Purpose
  • Standardized test scores (GRE/GMAT/ACT)
  • Official transcripts or certified copies
  • At least 3.0 GPA or grade C in previous degree
  • 2 academic and 1 professional LOR

Cost Of Studying In New Zealand

Tuition at New Zealand institutions usually ranges from NZ$25,000 to NZ$50,000 annually. Furthermore, MBA programs are frequently the most expensive, as they are in most other nations. These programs typically cost between $31,000 and NZD 50,000.

International students can live on campus in the Halls of Residence at several of New Zealand’s institutions. Besides, living on campus is a very cost-effective choice. The cost of living in different cities can differ significantly, but typical student budgets are manageable with proper planning.

Scholarships For International Students

When it comes to other wealthy nations, New Zealand has comparatively modest fees. Scholarships can be competitive, but some considering leadership potential, study field, and place of origin.

Scholarships are available from the New Zealand Government to qualifying nationals of developing nations, sponsored by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs & Trade New Zealand. Our team can assist you in identifying and applying for scholarships that best suit your needs.

Post-Study Work Opportunities

New Zealand maintains a progressive stance for overseas students. After graduating, you will be qualified to apply for a three-year open post-study work visa. During your studies, you can work a certain amount of hours.

The visa allows you to stay in New Zealand for up to three years to hunt for employment. You are not limited to a particular job or area and can work for nearly any business due to the open terms of your visa.
