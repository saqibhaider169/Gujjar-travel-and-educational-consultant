What Is Go8 Australia?

The top eight research-intensive Australian institutions are members of the Group of Eight (Go8). It began operations in 1999 and is made up of these eight prestigious institutions:

  • The University of Adelaide
  • The Australian National University
  • The University of Melbourne
  • Monash University
  • The University of New South Wales
  • The University of Queensland
  • The University of Sydney
  • The University of Western Australia

Admission Requirements In Australia

It’s simple to apply to Australian universities. Students must use caution while arranging paperwork and the GST procedure, too. While, all colleges have comparable basic requirements for overseas students seeking admission, which include:

  • A copy of a valid passport
  • Official academic records, such as transcripts and certificates
  • English language testing: PTE or IELTS
  • Recommendation letters
  • Specific Mission statement (Statement of Purpose)
  • A summary of your professional background, if applicable
  • Bank Statement with supporting documentation

How Much Does It Cost to Study In Australia?

Australia’s world-class universities and excellent educational system make it a popular choice for overseas students seeking higher education. However, the cost of attending an Australian university varies according to the school and subject selected. Undergraduate course fees usually range between 12,000 and 15,000 AUD each semester.

In addition to paying tuition, overseas students must cover living expenses such as housing, food, and transportation. Also, the average annual living expenditure for overseas students studying in Australia is estimated to be 21,041 AUD. Furthermore, students might also need to budget for travel expenditures, which can run up to $2,200 AUD.

However, the ability for overseas students to work up to 24 hours a week as a part-time employee is one benefit of studying in Australia. They can obtain work experience and use it to help pay for their housing and travel expenses.

Post-Study Work Opportunities

International students who choose to stay in Australia after finishing their studies have access to great post-study employment possibilities. While, after earning a bachelor’s degree, students can apply for a Post-Study Work (PSW) visa, which enables them to work and stay in Australia for two to four years, depending on the duration of their selected program of study.

Therefore, in a comparable manner, graduates of Master’s programs are eligible to apply for a three- to five-year-long PSW visa. Moreover, with the help of these PSW visas, overseas students can grow their careers in Australia, make money, and obtain useful job experience. Australia is a popular location for overseas students who want to pursue higher education and further their careers because of the post-study job options there.

Student Visa (Subclass 500)

You must have a student visa (subclass 500) in order to study in Australia. Below is a summary of the essential visa requirements:

General Specifications:

Enrolled in a registered course

To be eligible to use the Commonwealth Register of Institutions and Courses for Overseas Students (CRICOS), you must be enrolled full-time in a course that is registered.

Requirements for Genuine Temporary Entrants (GTE)

Show that you truly want to study in Australia and, after your studies are over, go back to your native country. Therefore, this may be proven by providing proof of your close links to your native nation, precise study goals, and ample financial resources.

Health Coverage for Overseas Students (OSHC)

Additionally, for the whole time you are visiting Australia, you’ll require to be covered by health insurance.

Fulfill the conditions for character and health

In addition, you must fulfill character requirements and pass health exams.

Documentation

  • Valid Passport
  • Completed Application Form
  • Confirmation of Enrolment
  • Proof of English Language Proficiency
  • Proof of Financial Capacity
  • Health Insurance
