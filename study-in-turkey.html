Why Study In Turkey?

Turkey’s public and private colleges follow the Bologna Agreement, which harmonizes degree programs throughout Europe. According to the most current QS Higher Education System Strength Rankings, Turkey’s higher education system is ranked 43rd in the world. Furthermore, it is home to over 200 universities, several state-owned.

In Turkey, universities exist in nearly every city! Social clubs, athletic teams, and extracurricular activities allow students to socialize and help them overcome geographic barriers at every school.

Admission Requirements In Turkey

Undergraduates Courses

  • YOS Exam: Mandatory standardization test for overseas students.
  • High School Equivalency Certificate: Prove your secondary education matches Turkish standards.

Graduate Courses (Direct Application)

Many universities allow direct applications for Master’s and PhD programs. Generally, you’ll need to submit:

  • Official Transcripts (Translated into English or Turkish)
  • Standardized Test Scores (GRE/GMAT)
  • Letter of Motivation
  • Letters of Recommendation (2 academic, 1 professional)

Cost Of Studying In Turkey

Tuition costs vary depending on the university and the subject. Public institutions often don’t charge more than US $1500 per year, while private schools may charge up to US $20,000 for an identical education.

Monthly Living Costs: Without rent, the average monthly cost of living in Istanbul is around 12,500 TRY (taqreeban US $1700).

Scholarships For International Students In Turkey

Several Turkish colleges provide merit-based scholarships that might pay for all or portion of the tuition:

  • Koç University Scholarships: Merit-based for Master’s and PhD levels.
  • Bilkent University Scholarships: Partial and full tuition waivers available.

Student Visa Requirements In Turkey

  • Valid Passport
  • Health insurance
  • Visa processing fee
  • Passport-size photos
  • Proof of flight tickets
  • University acceptance letter
  • Education certificates
  • Proof of sufficient funds

Post-Study Work Opportunities

Working in Turkey is permissible for international students enrolled in degree programs. International students can work up to 24 hours a week. Graduate students (Master's and PhD) can begin working as early as their first year.

Multinational corporations provide students with exposure to a multicultural setting through internship programs in both governmental and corporate sectors.
