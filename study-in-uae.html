Why Study In UAE?

Pursuing education in the UAE enables students to avail countless opportunities and high-quality education. Several universities in the UAE region rank high on the QS Ranking Index. The UAE offers accessible educational pathways, affordable living expenses, and access to top-notch facilities. Research is strongly encouraged to give students space for exploration and creativity, and universities welcome transfers.

The UAE also provides several advantages, including safe future investment, an inclusive atmosphere, financial aid, and one of the safest living environments in the world.

Admission Requirements In The UAE

  • Security form
  • High school certificate
  • Copy of valid passport
  • Medical examination form
  • Passport size photographs
  • Proof of financial statements
  • English proficiency certificate
  • Birth certificate (verifying age)

How Much Does it Cost to Study In the UAE?

Tuition Fees: Undergraduate tuition fees range between 37,500 to 70,000 AED annually. For postgraduate students, the average is approximately 55,000 to 75,000 AED.

Apart from tuition fees, students sustain themselves by taking responsibility for their personal expenses. Average monthly expenses for international students range from 650 to 1,300 EUR.

Scholarships For International Students In UAE

Even though tuition may be expensive, there are ways to pay for it. While many government scholarships are for residents, a few universities provide overseas students with scholarships for study. The scholarships may be partially or fully funded, according to the eligibility and academics of the applicant.

Student Visa Requirements In The UAE

  • Passport (Multiple copies)
  • Passport-sized pictures (up to 12)
  • University Admission Letter
  • Proof of financial solvency
  • Proof of accommodation
  • Medical certificate (HIV, TB, Hep B/C screening)
  • Previous certifications (for PG)

Post-Study Work Opportunities

The UAE offers excellent work prospects across energy, banking, and real estate sectors—mostly in Abu Dhabi and Dubai—thanks to the flourishing expat community and tax-free earnings.

Gold Visa: The UAE government has introduced a novel visa program for talented students where eligible students and families can be granted a 10-year resident visa.
