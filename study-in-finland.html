Finland is among the first nations that spring to mind when discussing top-notch education. As a Nordic nation with 5.54 million people as of 2024, it has done a remarkable job of both keeping up with and establishing worldwide standards in education.

Moreover, Finland has 35 higher education institutions and is a member of the EU, making it a popular destination for international students. Also, Finland provides an excellent social environment for learning and personal development since it is an egalitarian culture that places a high priority on knowledge and lifelong learning.

The ease of living in one of the safest nations in the world, the security of residing in Finland, and the advanced infrastructure and technology all contribute to the quality of life for international students.

Why Study In Finland?

Finland is a pleasant, human-scale nation whose towns and cities are made for people, not just automobiles. Rush hours are rare. Whether the universities are sufficiently small to run efficiently and successfully, there are many distinct learning settings to select from, from major metropolitan campuses to campuses next to nature. They are all internationally driven and have unique regional attributes.

Although universities enjoy much autonomy, the Ministry of Education and Culture provides most of their funding. As a result, the Ministry carefully monitors the standard of instruction. These well-run establishments react in their teaching and curriculum to the demands of business, industry, and society. They provide an extensive selection of excellent English-language courses for degree-seeking and exchange students at all educational levels.

Cost of studying in Finland

Fees for tuition:

Tuition costs for overseas students in undergraduate programs typically range from €5,000 to €18,000 per year. For master’s programs, the usual range is €11,000 to €20,000. There are several notable outliers, with some prestigious programs costing significantly more.

Living Costs & Accommodation:

Living expenditures are depending on your lifestyle preferences and the city in which you reside, living expenses might vary from €8,000 to €12,000 annually. Usually, the largest portion of your living costs goes towards accommodation. While, compared to private rents, student living and shared flats may be more economical.

Scholarships For International Students In Finland

Yes, there are several financial options available to students who wish to study abroad in Finland. Scholarships for universities are the easiest to apply for. A variety of scholarships are available to overseas students at every Finnish institution. These are frequently given to specific degree programs and are mostly determined by academic performance.

Make sure you research the scholarship opportunities available at each Finnish university before starting your application process. Our team of experts can assist you at every stage. Applicants can use our extensive database and online tools to choose the scholarship that best suits their needs.

Post-Study Work Opportunities

Students studying abroad in Finland have the option to work thirty hours per month in a part-time capacity. After you graduate, you can apply for a residence visa to start a business or look for work. Additionally, you may benefit from this two-year post-study permit for five years following graduation. If you’d like, you can even take it in parts.

Furthermore, international students find it easy to register and remain in Finland because their study-related stays contribute towards the four years required to be eligible for permanent residency. This makes the urge to go here faster and easier!
