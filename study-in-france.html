Why Study In France?

France has one of the largest higher education systems in Europe, if not the world. Numerous French colleges consistently hold prominent positions in global university rankings. École Normale Supérieure, École Polytechnique, Pierre and Marie Curie University, and the University of Paris-Sud are a few.

With its vibrant student population, rich culture, delicious cuisine, and stunning architecture, France is the perfect destination for your degree study abroad! Besides, you may acquire a new cultural perspective and learn the language by studying in France.

International students regard France as a desirable destination for study abroad degrees. French universities offer various class schedules and courses, and students can enroll full-time or part-time. The hours students can attend these courses will also vary depending on the college.

Admission Requirements In France

Admission requirements for studying in France can vary depending on the specific institution, program level (undergraduate vs. graduate), and your nationality (EU/EEA vs. non-EU/EEA). Here’s a general overview of the common steps and documents involved for non-EU/EEA students:

  • Choose Your Program: Research universities and programs that align with your academic interests.
  • Check Eligibility: Ensure you meet the basic entry requirements for your chosen program. This might include minimum academic qualifications (e.g., high school diploma or equivalent) and relevant subject prerequisites.
  • Apply for the DAP (Demande admission Préalable): This is a compulsory pre-admission application required for most non-EU/EEA students applying for Bachelor’s or Master’s programs in France. Applications are submitted through the platform “Mes Services” on the website of Campus France.

Application Documents:

  • Completed Online Application Form
  • Official transcripts
  • Standardized Test Scores (if required)
  • Proof of English or French Language Proficiency
  • Passport Copy
  • Motivation Letter
  • Letter of Recommendation
  • Portfolio
  • Financial Guarantee

Cost Of Studying In France

In addition to receiving a top-notch education, public colleges in France also provide very affordable tuition. Moreover, except for a matriculation fee of about 200 euros annually, citizens and permanent residents of EEA countries and Switzerland are eligible to study at public institutions without paying tuition.

However, students from countries other than Europe must pay between 3,000 and 4,000 euros annually. Besides, private colleges typically charge up to 20,000 euros each year. Public universities charge students from outside the EU (Bachelor’s, Master’s, or PhD) between 170 and 650 EUR a year, depending on the subject. Living as a student in France doesn’t have to be costly.

Additionally, in places like Toulouse, Lyon, or Bordeaux, the student budget is estimated to be 850–1,000 euros a month, including housing. Even so, it is modest by European standards. You may save even more money in smaller communities, as costs vary area-wise. However, Paris, the capital, is an exception; at the very least costs 1,250 to 1,400 euros each month.

Scholarships For International Students In France

Studying in France offers many perks that are likely to be recognized, especially combined with the exceptional institutions and learning opportunities in France. If a student wants to pursue higher education at a French institution, many financial aid options are available to make college more accessible.

In addition, students who meet the eligibility requirements can apply for grants and loans. A college education in France is a prudent financial decision because the French government also pays for a portion of the fees associated with attending a university in the nation. Besides, France has far cheaper tuition than other places.

Moreover, international students can fund their studies in France and around Europe with the support of several scholarships provided by French public institutes and the Erasmus+ program.

Post-Study Work Opportunities

For international graduates, there are several avenues for employment in France. Besides, your country may exempt you from requiring a temporary residency permit. After graduating, a student can only remain in France if he has paid employment. Moreover, students who are employed can apply for a residency permit. Graduate and undergraduate students alike should take note of this.

If a non-European graduate student is unemployed, they might apply for a temporary residency permit called an Autorisation Provisoire de Séjour. While studying in France, international students are also permitted to work at the university where they are staying. To make it easier for students to handle studies, jobs are arranged around study and class periods. Yet, this is an effective method of gaining job experience.
