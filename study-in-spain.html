Cost of Studying in Spain

The money question always comes first in our consultations, so let’s address it directly.

Tuition Fees: Public vs Private Universities

Public universities are where most international students end up, and for good reason:

  • Undergraduate programs: €750 to €2,500 per year depending on the region and credit load
  • Master’s programs: €1,000 to €4,000 per year

Spanish regions control their own education pricing, so Catalonia (Barcelona) typically charges more than Andalusia (Granada, Seville). The credit-based system means you pay per course, not a flat annual fee. Most bachelor’s degrees require 240 ECTS credits over four years; calculate your total cost by multiplying the per-credit rate by 60 credits annually.

Private universities charge differently:

  • Undergraduate programs: €5,000 to €20,000 per year
  • Master’s programs: €8,000 to €25,000 per year

Private institutions like IE University or ESADE Business School offer smaller class sizes and stronger corporate networks but at prices that start approaching UK levels.

Living Expenses: The Real Monthly Budget

Forget the €500/month budgets you might see online. Based on what our students actually spend, here’s the realistic breakdown:

Madrid or Barcelona (Major Cities):

  • Shared apartment: €350-€500/month
  • Food & groceries: €200-€300/month
  • Transportation pass: €40-€55/month
  • Mobile phone: €15-€25/month
  • Total: €900-€1,100/month

Valencia, Seville (Smaller Cities):

  • Shared apartment: €250-€400/month
  • Food & groceries: €180-€250/month
  • Transportation pass: €30-€45/month
  • Mobile phone: €15-€20/month
  • Total: €750-€950/month

Student dormitories (colegios mayores) cost €360 to €650 monthly and include meals, but availability is limited and application deadlines are strict. Most international students end up in shared private apartments.

Intakes in Spain Universities

Spain operates on a two-intake system that aligns with the European academic calendar:

Fall Intake (September–October Start)

This is the main intake with the most program availability. Application deadlines typically fall between January and May. Public universities often have earlier deadlines than private ones. The fall intake is more competitive because it attracts the highest number of applicants.

Spring Intake (January–February Start)

Less common and primarily available for master’s programs or specific bachelor’s programs at private universities. Application deadlines usually run from September to November. Spring intake classes tend to be smaller, but program choices are more limited.

Top Universities in Spain

Spanish universities consistently appear in global rankings and carry weight on a CV:

  • University of Barcelona: Ranked ~149th globally, strong in medicine & humanities
  • Pompeu Fabra University: known for economics & business
  • Autonomous University of Barcelona: excellent for engineering
  • Autonomous University of Madrid: particularly strong in law & sciences
  • Complutense University of Madrid: Spain’s largest and most historic
  • University of Navarra: private university with top medical school
  • University of Valencia: strong in STEM fields

Scholarships for International Students in Spain

Scholarships exist, but let’s be realistic about availability:

  • Spanish Government Scholarships (MAEC-AECID): Mostly for Master's/PhD (€1k-€1.2k stipend)
  • Erasmus+ / Erasmus Mundus: EU-funded, covers full tuition + large stipend
  • La Caixa Foundation: Highly competitive postgraduate scholarships
  • University Merit-Based: Partial waivers (25% - 75%) for top students

Reality Check

Most international students in Spain pay full tuition. Scholarships help, but building your budget around the assumption you’ll receive one is risky. Always have a backup financial plan.
